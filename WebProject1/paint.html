Different Paints:


Paint is any pigmented liquid that after application to a surface (wood,glass,paper,etc) in a thin layer, converts to a solid film.
Paint can be tricky to work with sometimes but it's super fun and very versatile! Some form of paints are:
  1. Oil Paint: This is a slow drying paint that has been mixed with oil to make it waterproof and long-lasting. The viscocity of the paint can change depending on how much paint you put on the surface area. It's a very beautiful medium that takes a little bit to master!
  2. Acrylic: a fast-drying paint made of pigment suspended in acrylic polymer emulsion and plasticizers, silicon oils, defoamers, stabilizers, or metal soaps.[2] Most acrylic paints are water-based, but become water-resistant when dry.
    Depending on how much the paint is diluted with water the finished acrylic painting can resemble a watercolor, a gouache, or an oil painting, or have its own unique characteristics not attainable with other media.
  3. Gouache: a water-medium, paint consisting of natural pigment, water, a binding agent and sometimes additional inert material.
    Gouache is designed to be opaque. Gouache has been used for a long time! It's like a thicker water-color.
  4. Pastel: an art medium in the form of a stick, consisting of powdered pigment and a binder. The pigments used in pastels are similar to those used to produce some other colored visual arts media, such as oil paints; the binder is of a neutral hue and low saturation.
    The color effect of pastels is closer to the natural dry pigments than that of any other process. It may be weird,but this form of painting is called "dry painting"!
